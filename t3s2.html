Step Two

Paragraph structure: Flip your paragraphs

Often, when I'm writing a first draft, I start a paragraph asking a question, and by the last sentence of the paragraph I've answered it. While this might feel like the most natural way to write, it isn't usually the easiest for readers to follow. So I take each paragraph, and flip it to create a paragraph with a good topic sentence. Here's how:

To Do:

Re-read your paragraphs. Do the first sentence or two give the reader an idea of what to expect? If not, take a look at the last sentence. Does it provide a better summary of what the paragraph does? Great. Move that sentence to the beginning of your paragraph.

Strategies:

  • Go back to your reverse outline. If you don't think your paragraph has a topic sentence, look at the reverse outline you created in the previous step, and see if you can adapt the summary you wrote of that paragraph to create a topic sentence.
  • Focus each paragraph. Again, if you have a hard time characterizing the paragraph in one sentence, that's a red flag. You may need to break the paragraph down into its component parts, or figure out if it's really relevant to your argument.
