Step Four

Does your thesis have a clear focus?

Another way your thesis can be specific is by narrowing your focus to address a small set of evidence. If you're writing an essay about a novel, a good thesis might narrow your focus to a few scenes, a set of images, or a single relationship. Often, the more you "zoom in" while developing the argument, the stronger it will be.

To Do:

Ask yourself these questions:

  • Does your thesis gesture towards a specific and narrow body of evidence that you will analyze to prove your point? If not, figure out how you will prove your thesis and whether the amount of evidence you will need to examine is manageable.
  • Does your thesis take on one main question, or is it trying to answer multiple, unrelated ones? If you can break your thesis down into unrelated parts, you should pick just one and focus on it.

Example:

Our thesis from the last step might be made stronger by relating it to a specific episode or scene.

More focused:"The cinematic techniques used in two death scenes in Episode 9 of Game of Thrones subvert audience expectations and fantasy tropes, and in doing so serve as a critique of the way the genre has desensitized its audience to violence."
